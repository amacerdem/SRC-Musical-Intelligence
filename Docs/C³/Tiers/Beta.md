# Beta Tier -- Integrative Models

**Tier Symbol**: beta
**Evidence Standard**: 70--90% confidence, 5 <= k < 10 studies, cross-domain synthesis
**Count**: 40 models (variable per unit: 3--9)

---

## Definition

Beta-tier models represent the integrative layer of the C3 architecture. They synthesize findings across related domains to build cross-modal, cross-system, or cross-species models that extend beyond the direct mechanistic mappings of alpha models. Beta models have moderate-to-strong empirical support but rely on fewer independent studies and may involve inference across methodological boundaries.

Beta models satisfy the following evidence criteria:

| Criterion | Requirement |
|-----------|------------|
| Confidence range | 70--90% (typically 0.70--0.89) |
| Study count | 5 <= k < 10 independent studies |
| Effect sizes | Reported but may be from heterogeneous paradigms |
| Replication | Partial replication or convergent evidence across methods |
| Neural evidence | May include correlational neuroimaging, behavioral studies, or computational modeling |
| Falsifiability | At least one explicit falsification criterion declared |

---

## Characteristics of Beta Models

1. **Standard output dimensionality**: Beta models typically produce 10--12D per frame.
2. **Cross-domain integration**: Many beta models bridge between domains (e.g., music-language, music-movement, perception-action).
3. **Growing evidence base**: These models are actively being validated; many are candidates for promotion to alpha as new studies accumulate.
4. **Variable count per unit**: Unlike the uniform 3-alpha-per-unit structure, beta model counts vary from 3 to 9, reflecting differential maturity across research domains.

## Promotion and Demotion

Beta models may be promoted to alpha or demoted to gamma based on evolving evidence:

**Promotion to alpha** requires:
- Accumulation of k >= 10 independent studies
- Confidence range exceeding 0.90
- At least one direct replication study
- Consistent effect sizes across paradigms

**Demotion to gamma** occurs when:
- Key supporting studies fail to replicate
- Effective paper count drops below 5
- Confidence range falls below 0.70
- Fundamental assumptions are challenged by new evidence

## Evidence Standards

To qualify for beta tier, a model's `ModelMetadata` must satisfy:

```python
@dataclass(frozen=True)
class ModelMetadata:
    evidence_tier: str = "beta"            # Must be "beta"
    confidence_range: Tuple[float, float]   # 0.70 <= low, high <= 0.90
    paper_count: int                        # 5 <= k < 10
    falsification_criteria: Tuple[str, ...]  # At least one criterion
```
