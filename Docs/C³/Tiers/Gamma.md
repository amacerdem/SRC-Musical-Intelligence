# Gamma Tier -- Theoretical Models

**Tier Symbol**: gamma
**Evidence Standard**: <70% confidence, k < 5 studies, theory-driven
**Count**: 29 models (3 per unit x 7 units + 5 for STU)

---

## Definition

Gamma-tier models represent the theoretical frontier of the C3 architecture. They encode hypotheses derived from limited empirical data, computational modeling, or extrapolation from adjacent domains. Gamma models are the most speculative components of the system -- they capture phenomena that are theoretically important but lack sufficient empirical validation for higher tier classification.

Gamma models satisfy the following evidence criteria:

| Criterion | Requirement |
|-----------|------------|
| Confidence range | <70% (typically 0.50--0.69) |
| Study count | k < 5 independent studies |
| Effect sizes | May be absent, preliminary, or from pilot studies |
| Replication | No direct replication; may rely on single studies |
| Neural evidence | May include case studies, animal models, computational predictions |
| Falsifiability | At least one explicit falsification criterion declared |

---

## Characteristics of Gamma Models

1. **Minimal output dimensionality**: Gamma models uniformly produce 10D per frame, reflecting conservative parameterization given limited evidence.
2. **Theory-driven**: Many gamma models are derived from computational theory or extrapolation rather than direct observation.
3. **Higher revision risk**: These models are most likely to be restructured, renamed, or replaced as new evidence emerges.
4. **Exploratory scope**: Gamma models often address emerging topics (developmental effects, cross-species comparisons, individual differences, clinical applications).
5. **Validation priority**: Each gamma model defines specific falsification criteria and empirical tests that could promote it to beta tier.

## Common Themes in Gamma Models

Gamma models cluster around several recurring research frontiers:

1. **Developmental**: DAP, DMMS, SDDP, PTGMP -- how musical processing changes across the lifespan.
2. **Individual Differences**: LDAC, IOTMS, IGFE, MPFS -- why people respond differently to the same music.
3. **Clinical/Therapeutic**: TAR, ONI, NSCP -- applications of musical processing models to therapy and intervention.
4. **Cross-species**: CSSL -- evolutionary origins of musical cognition.
5. **Expertise Effects**: ESME, MTNE, ECT, STC -- how musical training reshapes neural circuits.
6. **Computational Theory**: PWSM, PSH, SSPS -- predictions from formal models awaiting empirical test.
7. **Cross-modal**: CMAT, CDEM -- transfer of musical processing to other sensory domains.

## Promotion to Beta

Gamma models may be promoted to beta tier when evidence accumulates. Promotion requires:

- Accumulation of k >= 5 independent studies
- Confidence range exceeding 0.70
- At least one replication or convergent evidence from a different methodology
- Reported effect sizes consistent with the model's predictions

Each gamma model declares specific `falsification_criteria` in its `ModelMetadata`. These criteria define the empirical results that would either promote the model (if confirmed) or eliminate it (if contradicted).

---

## Risk Assessment

Gamma models carry the highest revision risk in the architecture:

| Risk Level | Description | Gamma Models Affected |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| Low | Well-motivated theory, evidence emerging | PWSM, EDTA, CDEM |
| Medium | Plausible but untested in music domain | CSSL, CTBB, IGFE |
| High | Speculative, single-study basis | MPFS, SSPS, ONI |

Models assessed as high-risk are flagged for priority empirical investigation. If disconfirmed, they will be removed from the architecture rather than retained at gamma tier.

## Evidence Standards

To qualify for gamma tier, a model's `ModelMetadata` must satisfy:

```python
@dataclass(frozen=True)
class ModelMetadata:
    evidence_tier: str = "gamma"           # Must be "gamma"
    confidence_range: Tuple[float, float]   # low < 0.70
    paper_count: int                        # k < 5
    falsification_criteria: Tuple[str, ...]  # At least one criterion
```
